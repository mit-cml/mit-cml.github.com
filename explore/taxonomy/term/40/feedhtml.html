We address the challenges of applying smartphone data to improve the<br /> experiences of teaching and learning in the university environment. Specifically, we cre-<br /> ated a framework to collect, monitor and analyze student behavioral data and increase<br /> social interactions for educational uses. The framework has three essential components.<br /> The first component is App Inventor, a web-based platform with drag-and-drop tools<br /> for novices to create mobile apps that collect personal and environmental data. The sec-<br /> ond component is Reactive Data Store, an intuitive mechanism that connects multiple<br /> data sources and automates the tasks for analyzing personal data. The third compo-<br /> nent is a push notification mechanism that delivers personalized or group messages as<br /> the results of the real-time analysis and triggers more actions accordingly. Together,<br /> these components will transform people's campus experiences by unleashing the values<br /> of smartphone data. Lastly, we introduced the framework to a group of teachers in<br /> higher education with a focus group discussion to identify the plausible use cases in<br /> terms of performance assessments, pedagogy design and collaborative learning.<br /> In Book Title: Smart University Workshop in Eighth International and Interdisciplinary Conference on Modeling and Using Context, November 2013 </p> </div></div></div><section class="field field-name-field-resource-files field-type-file field-label-above view-mode-rss"><h2 class="field-label">Files:&nbsp;</h2><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><span class="file"><img class="file-icon" alt="" title="application/pdf" src="/modules/file/icons/application-pdf.png" /> <a href="http://explore.appinventor.mit.edu/sites/explore.appinventor.mit.edu/files/resources/smartUniv.pdf" type="application/pdf;
